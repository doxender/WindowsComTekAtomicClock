ComTek Atomic Clock — Windows
Requirements specification
==========================================

Last updated: 2026-04-25

Note: this file is a project requirements document, not a Python pip
manifest. .NET dependencies are declared in each project's .csproj file.
This document is the source of truth for what the product must do; it
is updated whenever a scope, architecture, or tooling decision changes.

------------------------------------------------------------------
1. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1  Clock display
     - The product ships with eleven face themes (subject to revision)
       organized into three categories. Visual references and design
       rationale for each live under design/themes/ in this repo.

       (a) Analog with integrated digital readout:
           - Atomic Lab    (default on first run)
           - Boulder Slate
           - Aero Glass
           - Cathode
           - Concourse
           - Daylight

       (b) Digital-only:
           - Flip Clock      (retro mechanical, animated card flip)
           - Marquee         (theater bulbs, animated chase)
           - Slab            (brutalist concrete, static)
           - Binary Digital  (binary HH/MM/SS as text, terminal
                              aesthetic; pairs with the BCD encoding
                              face below for users who want the
                              binary read-out without the dot grid)

       (c) Specialty encodings:
           - Binary      (BCD LED stack with decoded readout)
           - Hex         (hex per unit + 16-bit day fraction shown
                          as a literal #RRGGBB color swatch)

     - The active theme is selectable per clock window and per tab
       (see § 1.2 for the per-tab settings gear and § 1.8 for the
       global "same theme everywhere" override).
     - Analog second-hand motion default is theme-decided. Each
       analog theme picks `smooth` (sub-second sweep) or `stepped`
       (once-per-second tick) as part of its identity; per-theme
       defaults are documented in design/README.md. Each tab and
       free-floating window may override the default via a
       three-way `Theme default / Smooth / Stepped` selector
       exposed in the settings popover (§ 1.2, § 1.8).
     - Theme animations beyond the second hand — Flip Clock card
       flips, Marquee bulb chase, Binary BCD dot glow on toggle,
       Hex day-fraction color drift — are always rendered when the
       Windows system "Show animations in Windows" accessibility
       setting is enabled, and always omitted when it is disabled.
       There is no app-level animation toggle; the system
       accessibility setting is the single source of truth.
     - Per-theme color customization. Every theme — analog,
       digital-only, AND encoding — exposes the same five
       user-overridable color slots, set per tab (or per
       free-floating window) via the settings gear popover (§ 1.2):

         · Ring      - outermost frame / bezel / case
         · Face      - dial background / panel / tile face
         · Hands     - hour + minute + second hands (analog) or
                       primary accent strokes (digital, encoding)
         · Numbers   - numerals (analog) or primary big digits
                       (digital, encoding)
         · Digital   - integrated digital readout color (analog) or
                       secondary/auxiliary digital text such as
                       seconds, decoded readout, footer captions
                       (digital, encoding)

       Each theme's renderer maps the five slots to the closest
       visual element for that theme; every theme implements all
       five slots so the settings UI is identical across theme
       types. design/README.md documents the per-theme
       slot-to-element mapping. The settings popover shall offer a
       "Reset colors to theme defaults" action.

       Theme-specific accent colors that do not map to any of the
       five slots (e.g., Boulder Slate's red second-hand disc,
       Atomic Lab's `NIST · BOULDER · CO` legend, Flip Clock's
       amber colon pips) remain at the theme's discretion and are
       NOT user-overridable. These accents are part of each theme's
       identity.
     - Faces shall update at least once per second; analog second
       hand motion may be smooth (sub-second) or stepped at the
       user's choice.
     - For analog themes (category a), the integrated digital readout
       shall be independently hideable per clock window or per tab,
       leaving a pure analog face. The toggle is hidden for themes
       in categories (b) and (c) since they have no analog face to
       reveal.
     - 24-hour vs 12-hour rendering. The time format setting
       (per-tab, per-window — see § 1.8) is one of `Auto` (follow
       Windows locale, the default), `12h`, or `24h`.

         12-hour mode renders the digital readout as `H:MM:SS AM/PM`
         (or as `H:MM:SS` with a separate AM/PM indicator on themes
         where the readout is space-constrained). Analog numeral
         positions render their canonical labels (1-12 or
         12/3/6/9 depending on theme).

         24-hour mode renders the digital readout as `HH:MM:SS`,
         no AM/PM. On analog themes, the dial keeps its 12 numeral
         positions but the labels SWAP at the 12:00:00 boundaries:
           - From midnight (00:00:00) to noon (12:00:00):
             positions show their AM labels (1, 2, ..., 11, 12 for
             full-numeral themes; 12, 3, 6, 9 for sparse themes).
           - From noon (12:00:00) to midnight (24:00:00):
             positions 1-11 show 13-23; position 12 stays as "12";
             sparse themes flip 3->15, 6->18, 9->21 with 12 fixed.
           - The flip is abrupt at the exact second boundary, not
             animated. Boulder Slate has no numerals and is
             unaffected.

1.2  Multiple time zones (tabbed view)
     - The main application window shall present a tabbed view, each
       tab bound to a single IANA time zone (e.g., `America/New_York`,
       `Europe/Berlin`, `Asia/Tokyo`). Resolved at runtime via
       .NET 8's `TimeZoneInfo.FindSystemTimeZoneById` which accepts
       IANA IDs natively on Windows.
     - Each tab shall expose a settings gear icon on the right side
       of the tab header. Clicking it opens a popover containing:
         - Time-zone selector. A type-to-search filter over the full
           IANA catalog (~600 zones). Typing partial city or region
           names ("tok" → Tokyo, Tokelau; "berlin" → Europe/Berlin)
           narrows the list. Stored in settings.json as the IANA
           string (e.g., `"timezone": "America/New_York"`).
         - Theme selector (dropdown of all themes from § 1.1). This
           selector is disabled when the global "Use the same theme
           across all tabs" toggle is ON (see § 1.8).
         - "Show digital readout" toggle. Visible only when the
           active theme is in category (a). Hidden for digital-only
           and specialty themes since they have no separable digital
           readout to toggle.
         - Five color pickers labeled "Ring", "Face", "Hands",
           "Numbers", and "Digital" (per § 1.1). Each picker, when
           clicked, opens a modern color-picker control containing:
             · HSV color wheel (hue + saturation/value).
             · RGB sliders (8-bit per channel).
             · Alpha (transparency) slider. Required for the
               desktop overlay mode (§ 1.4) and for translucent
               theme elements (e.g., Aero Glass acrylic).
             · Hex input field accepting `#RRGGBB` or `#RRGGBBAA`.
             · Eyedropper that picks a color from anywhere on
               screen.
           Each picker overrides the active theme's color for that
           slot on this tab only — unless the global "Use the same
           theme across all tabs" toggle is ON, in which case the
           override propagates to all tabs. A "Reset colors to
           theme defaults" link sits under the pickers and restores
           the theme's defaults for all five slots.
         - Time format selector: `Auto` / `12h` / `24h`. `Auto`
           (the default) follows the Windows locale's short-time
           format. See § 1.1 for the analog numeral-flip behavior
           in 24h mode.
         - Second-hand motion override: `Theme default` / `Smooth`
           / `Stepped`. Default is `Theme default`. Visible only on
           analog themes (§ 1.1 a) since digital and encoding
           themes have no analog second hand.
     - The user can add, rename, reorder, and remove tabs.

1.3  Multiple simultaneous clock windows
     - The user can spawn any number of additional clock windows.
     - Each window is independently configurable for time zone, face
       style, size, and on-top behavior.
     - Window state (position, size, time zone, face) shall persist
       across application restarts.

1.4  Desktop overlay mode
     - A clock window can be placed in "desktop overlay" mode: borderless,
       transparent background, optionally click-through, and pinned to
       the desktop layer (behind normal windows, in front of wallpaper).
     - Multiple overlay clocks may run simultaneously.

1.5  Atomic clock synchronization
     - The application shall synchronize the Windows system clock to
       a NIST time server. Default primary: `time.nist.gov` (anycast
       to NIST's stratum-1 pool; NIST's primary atomic clocks live
       in Boulder, CO).
     - Synchronization uses SNTP (RFC 4330) over UDP/123.
     - Default sync interval: hourly. User-configurable between
       15 minutes and 24 hours.
     - Manual "Sync now" action shall be available from the tray
       menu.
     - The Service shall respect the NIST minimum-poll-interval
       rule: no more than one query per server per 4 seconds, both
       within a single sync attempt and across the periodic
       interval.

     - Fallback pool (NIST-only). When the primary `time.nist.gov`
       anycast endpoint fails (timeout, DNS failure, refused
       packet), the Service walks NIST's published stratum-1 pool
       in a randomized order. The pool, hardcoded in the binary
       and refreshed per release from
       https://tf.nist.gov/tf-cgi/servers.cgi, is at minimum:

         Gaithersburg, MD:   time-a-g.nist.gov
                             time-b-g.nist.gov
                             time-c-g.nist.gov
                             time-d-g.nist.gov
                             time-e-g.nist.gov
         Fort Collins, CO:   time-a-wwv.nist.gov
                             time-b-wwv.nist.gov
                             time-c-wwv.nist.gov
                             time-d-wwv.nist.gov
                             time-e-wwv.nist.gov

       Each server is queried at most once per sync attempt; the
       first successful, signature-validated response is used.
     - No non-NIST fallback. If every NIST server in the pool fails
       in a single sync attempt — total NIST outage, network down,
       DNS broken — the Service logs a Warning to the Event Log
       (with the per-server failure reason), sleeps one full sync
       interval, and retries. The application is deliberately
       constrained to atomic time from the NIST pool; it will not
       silently degrade to `time.windows.com`, `pool.ntp.org`, or
       any other non-stratum-1 source.
     - The user-configurable "Sync server" setting (per § 1.8)
       accepts only hostnames within the NIST pool. Non-NIST
       hostnames are rejected at the input field with an
       explanatory tooltip ("ComTek Atomic Clock syncs only to the
       NIST stratum-1 pool. To use a different source, you'd be
       running a different clock.").

1.6  Background service
     - System clock synchronization shall run as a Windows Service so
       that it continues without an interactive user session.
     - Service start mode: Automatic.
     - Service shall recover automatically from transient network or
       DNS failures (exponential backoff, capped retry).

1.7  System tray integration
     - The UI shall provide a tray icon with: open main window, sync
       now, last-sync status, settings, exit.
     - Closing the last visible window shall minimize to tray, not
       terminate the UI process.

1.8  Settings
     - Per-tab: time zone, theme, "show digital readout" (analog
       themes only), time format (auto / 12h / 24h), second-hand
       motion override (theme default / smooth / stepped, analog
       themes only), and the five color overrides (ring, face,
       hands, numbers, digital).
     - Per-window (clock windows spawned outside the main tabbed
       view per § 1.3): time zone, theme, size, position, on-top,
       overlay mode, "show digital readout" (analog themes only),
       time format (auto / 12h / 24h), second-hand motion
       override, and the five color overrides.
     - Global:
         - Sync server, sync interval.
         - "Confirm large sync corrections" toggle (default OFF;
           when ON, corrections greater than the threshold prompt
           the user via toast — see § 2.5).
         - Large-offset threshold in seconds (default 5).
         - "Send anonymous crash reports" toggle (default OFF; see
           § 2.11).
         - "Send anonymous usage stats" toggle (default OFF; see
           § 2.11).
         - Start with Windows.
         - Service install state.
         - "Use the same theme across all tabs" toggle. When ON,
           changing the theme — or any of its five color overrides
           — on any tab applies to all tabs, and disables the
           per-tab theme and color selectors in their settings
           popovers. When OFF (the default), each tab keeps its own
           theme and its own color overrides. The toggle does NOT
           govern free-floating clock windows from § 1.3 — those
           are always per-window.
         - Default theme on first run: Atomic Lab.

1.9  Service-absent behavior (degraded mode)
     - When the Service is uninstalled, disabled, stopped, or
       otherwise unreachable over the named pipe (§ 2.4), the UI
       shall continue to run. All clock faces continue to display
       the Windows system clock for their bound time zone — the
       time just isn't being NIST-synced.
     - Every visible clock face shall surface the "service not
       running" warning directly on the face. The text replacement
       is per-category:

       - Analog themes (§ 1.1 a): the integrated digital readout
         text is replaced with the literal string
         "SERVICE NOT RUNNING". The analog hands continue showing
         the local time normally.
       - Digital-only themes (§ 1.1 b): the primary big-digit text
         is replaced with "SERVICE NOT RUNNING". The time is not
         displayed on these themes while the Service is down — the
         warning is the priority. (Users who need to see time
         during an outage can switch to an analog theme on the
         affected tab.)
       - Specialty encoding themes (§ 1.1 c): the decoded readout
         text below the encoded representation is replaced with
         "SERVICE NOT RUNNING". The encoded representation
         (BCD dots, hex digits, binary digits) continues to show
         the time.

     - The replacement text shall be drawn in the active theme's
       "Ring" slot color (per § 1.1) so it reads as obviously
       different from normal-time text and contrasts with the
       theme's Face color.
     - The main application window shall additionally show a banner
       with a button to enable the Service. The button's label
       reflects the detected Service state (the UI can enumerate
       local services as a standard user, no elevation required):

         - Service not installed:
             "Install and start the time-sync service"
         - Service installed but stopped:
             "Start the time-sync service"
         - Service running:
             (banner is not shown in this state)

       Clicking the button shells to a small privileged helper
       (`ComTekAtomicClock.ServiceInstaller.exe`, packaged
       alongside the UI). Windows fires the UAC consent prompt
       once. The helper re-detects Service state at run time
       (state may have changed between UI detection and click)
       and behaves as follows:

         - Not installed: runs
             sc.exe create ComTekAtomicClockSvc
               binPath= "<resolved path to Service.exe>"
               start= auto
               DisplayName= "ComTek Atomic Clock — time sync"
           followed by `sc.exe start ComTekAtomicClockSvc`.
         - Installed but stopped: runs only
             sc.exe start ComTekAtomicClockSvc
           (skips `create` — the existing service binary path,
            start mode, and display name are left untouched).
         - Already running: no-op, reports success, exits.

       Helper exits with code 0 on success, non-zero on any
       failure. The UI surfaces a non-zero exit as a toast/error
       dialog with the helper's stderr text.
     - When the Service comes back online, the on-face warning text
       reverts to normal time display, and the main-window banner
       dismisses automatically.

1.10 First-run experience
     - On first launch (no settings.json at the path in § 2.10), the
       application creates settings.json with the following defaults
       and opens the main window:
         - One tab bound to the system's local IANA time zone.
         - Atomic Lab theme.
         - Time format = Auto.
         - Sync interval = hourly. Sync server = `time.nist.gov`.
         - No color overrides; theme defaults are used for all five
           slots.
         - "Confirm large sync corrections" = OFF.
         - "Use the same theme across all tabs" = OFF.
     - There is no welcome dialog, no setup wizard, no tutorial.
       Discovery happens through use; the settings gear popover and
       tray menu are sufficient.
     - The Service install state is naturally surfaced by the § 1.9
       service-absent banner, which is showing when the app opens
       on a fresh non-MSIX install. The user installs the Service
       by clicking it; if they decline, the app continues to work
       — only NIST sync is unavailable until they enable the
       Service. The MSIX installer (§ 2.7) installs and starts the
       Service as part of package install, so the banner is not
       shown at first launch in that path.
     - The app shall NOT require any privileged action to reach a
       usable, time-displaying state.

------------------------------------------------------------------
2. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1  Platform
     - Windows 10 version 1809 or newer, and Windows 11.
     - x64 and ARM64 builds.

2.2  Runtime and language
     - C# 12 on .NET 8 (LTS).
     - WPF for the UI; Worker Service template for the system service;
       a class library for shared IPC contracts and models.

2.3  Privileges
     - The UI process shall run as the standard user.
     - Only the service requires LocalSystem (to call SetSystemTime).
     - The UI shall NOT request elevation for normal operation.

2.4  Inter-process communication
     - UI <-> Service via named pipe with a versioned message contract.
     - Authentication: pipe ACL restricts access to interactive users
       on the local machine.

2.5  Reliability
     - Service shall log sync attempts, offsets applied, and failures
       to the Windows Event Log under a custom source.
     - Sync offset greater than a configurable threshold (default 5 s)
       is logged at Warning. If the user has opted in to "Confirm
       large sync corrections" (per § 1.8), the Service shall NOT
       apply the correction immediately; instead it shall:
         - Notify the UI over the named pipe (§ 2.4) with the signed
           offset and the source server hostname.
         - The UI shall raise a Windows ToastNotification (Win10+
           framework, already a target platform per § 2.1) of the
           form:
             "Time off by 47 s (gain). Apply? [Apply] [Skip]"
           with the actual signed offset and direction substituted.
         - On `Apply`: UI sends Apply over the pipe; Service writes
           the new system time via `SetSystemTime` and logs an
           Information entry with the pre/post offset.
         - On `Skip`, or no response within 30 seconds (the user is
           afk): the Service skips this sync, logs an Information
           entry, and retries on the next sync interval. The clock
           remains drifted until the user accepts a future
           correction.
       When the "Confirm large sync corrections" toggle is OFF
       (the default), large corrections are applied immediately and
       the only user-visible signal is the Warning-level Event Log
       entry.

2.6  Security
     - All NIST traffic is plain SNTP; the service shall validate the
       NTP packet's mode, version, and stratum fields and reject
       malformed responses.
     - Release binaries shall be Authenticode-signed before
       distribution.

2.7  Packaging and updates
     - Single MSIX installer that deploys the UI, the Service, and
       the privileged installer helper (§ 1.9). MSIX registers the
       Service for automatic start at boot. The package bundles
       both x64 and ARM64 architectures (per § 2.1).
     - Uninstaller stops and removes the Service, removes the
       privileged helper, and leaves `settings.json` (§ 2.10) in
       place under `%APPDATA%\ComTekAtomicClock\`. Users frequently
       uninstall and reinstall to fix issues; preserving settings
       on uninstall is the user-friendly default.
     - Updates are delivered via an `.appinstaller` manifest file
       hosted on GitHub Pages:
         - URL pattern:
             https://doxender.github.io/WindowsComTekAtomicClock/
                 ComTekAtomicClock.appinstaller
         - Files served from the same Pages site:
             ComTekAtomicClock.appinstaller   - manifest pointing at
                                                the latest .msix
             ComTekAtomicClock-X.Y.Z.msix     - versioned package
                                                (prior versions kept
                                                in the repo for
                                                history but not
                                                referenced by the
                                                manifest)
         - First install: user clicks the .appinstaller link from a
           GitHub Release page or the README. Windows imports the
           manifest, installs the package, and registers the
           auto-update channel.
         - Subsequent versions: when a new .msix is referenced by
           the manifest, Windows detects the change on app launch
           and updates silently in the background. The user does
           not see an update prompt.
         - The .appinstaller is configured with
           `HoursBetweenUpdateChecks = 0` so an update check fires
           on every launch.
     - Release MSIX packages shall be Authenticode-signed before
       being published to the Pages site (per § 2.6). Unsigned MSIX
       is rejected by Windows on install.

2.8  Build environment
     - Hard prerequisite: .NET 8 SDK on PATH. The full solution shall
       build, test, and run from the command line via `dotnet build`
       / `dotnet run` with no IDE present.
     - Recommended for human inspection / debugging: Visual Studio
       2026 (or 2022) Community edition with the ".NET desktop
       development" workload. The IDE provides the XAML designer,
       multi-process debugging, and the Windows SDK / `signtool.exe`
       used during release packaging. The IDE is NOT required for
       contributors who only edit code.

     - Copyright (c) 2026 Daniel V. Oxender.
     - Source repository: https://github.com/doxender/WindowsComTekAtomicClock

2.10 Persistence and storage
     - All UI settings (per-tab, per-window, global, including the
       five-color overrides per active theme — see § 1.8) shall
       persist to a single JSON file at:

         %APPDATA%\ComTekAtomicClock\settings.json

       Per-user (Roaming AppData), portable between machines (the
       user can copy the file), and hand-editable.
     - Writes shall be atomic: write to a sibling `.tmp` file,
       flush to disk, then rename over the existing file. Avoids
       partial-write corruption on crash or power loss.
     - JSON schema includes a `schemaVersion` integer for forward
       migration. Unknown fields are preserved on read so a newer
       app version does not lose data when the file is later read
       by an older app version.
     - Created on first run if absent, populated with defaults
       (Atomic Lab theme, hourly sync, no extra tabs, single tab
       bound to the system's local time zone).
     - The Service has its own per-machine config at:

         %ProgramData%\ComTekAtomicClock\service.json

       (NOT in the user's %APPDATA% — the Service runs as
       LocalSystem, which has a different AppData root than any
       interactive user. %ProgramData% is shared across all users
       and across the Service's identity.)
     - The Service reads sync server, sync interval, "confirm large
       sync corrections" toggle, large-offset threshold, and Event
       Log source name from `service.json`. The UI writes this file
       whenever those settings change. Until the UI has written it
       at least once, the Service falls back to hardcoded defaults
       (`time.nist.gov`, 1-hour interval, threshold 5 s).
     - The MSIX installer (and the development-time
       `ComTekAtomicClock.ServiceInstaller` per § 1.9) creates
       `%ProgramData%\ComTekAtomicClock\` with NTFS ACLs that grant
       all interactive Users read/write on the directory so any
       user's UI process can update `service.json` without
       elevation.

2.11 Telemetry and crash reporting
     - Two opt-in toggles in global settings (§ 1.8), both default
       OFF and independent:
         - "Send anonymous crash reports"
         - "Send anonymous usage stats"
     - When the user toggles either to ON for the first time, a
       confirmation dialog summarizes the data that will be
       collected, links to the privacy policy
       (`PRIVACY.md` at repo root), and confirms consent.
       Subsequent toggles between ON and OFF do not re-prompt.

     - Crash reports include only:
         · Stack trace
         · Exception type and message
         · App version (semver)
         · Windows version (major.minor)
         · .NET runtime version
         · Local timestamp (no time-zone offset)
         · Active theme at time of crash

       Crash reports do NOT include: user names, file paths outside
       the app's own install directory, machine names, IP addresses
       (the receiving endpoint must not log them), settings values
       of any kind.

     - Usage stats include only (sampled, aggregated):
         · App launch count per session
         · Active theme(s) in use (count per theme)
         · Sync attempt count, success count, mean offset (signed)
         · Sync interval distribution
         · Theme-switch count per session
         · Tab count distribution
         · Free-floating window count distribution
         · Time format distribution (`Auto` / `12h` / `24h`)
         · Hide-digital readout usage rate (analog themes)

       Usage stats do NOT include: time zones in use (potential
       location hint), specific color override values, any
       free-form text the user has entered, any data tied to a
       persistent identifier.

     - Endpoint and infrastructure are a deferred decision. Pre-1.0
       options under consideration:
         · Self-hosted Sentry / GlitchTip for crashes.
         · Self-hosted Plausible / PostHog for usage stats.
         · Cloud Sentry free tier (~5k events/mo).
         · Custom Cloudflare Workers endpoint backed by R2.
       The privacy policy must accurately describe the chosen
       endpoint(s) and their retention behavior at release time.

     - All transmitted data shall use TLS 1.2 or newer.
     - The user may revoke consent at any time by toggling either
       category OFF; the next app launch stops sending data in that
       category. Already-sent data cannot be recalled — this is
       documented explicitly in the privacy policy.

     - Pre-public-release blocker: `PRIVACY.md` at the repo root,
       drafted to match the chosen telemetry infrastructure and
       reviewed for legal sufficiency. Tracked as an open
       pre-release item (similar to Tromp's pre-publication
       checklist).

3. OUT OF SCOPE (initial release)
------------------------------------------------------------------

     - Cross-platform builds (macOS, Linux).
     - Stratum-1 hardware reference clocks.
     - Authenticated NTP (NTS / symmetric keys).
     - Cloud sync of user settings across machines.
